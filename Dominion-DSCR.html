Introduction

Introduction

Purpose of Guidelines

These guidelines establish the criteria for originating residential investor loans under Dominion Financial, focusing exclusively on business-purpose investment properties.

Scope and Applicability

Applicable to all new loan applications submitted on or after the effective date, these guidelines cover single-family residences (SFR) and 2–9 Unit properties intended for investment purposes only.

2

Compliance Regulations

Loan Purpose

Dominion does not originate consumer loans. Dominion is required to ensure that all loans are not made for personal, family or household purposes. Borrowers are required to sign a business purpose affidavit or similar document which confirms that the loan purpose complies with this paragraph.

Bank Secrecy and USA PATRIOT Acts

The Bank Secrecy Act (BSA) requires financial institutions to assist US government agencies to detect and prevent money laundering. Dominion has implemented anti-money laundering policies and procedures to comply with applicable federal law. As a part of this policy, Dominion must screen all borrowers and guarantors against the list of specially designated nationals maintained by the US Department of the Treasury and OFAC pursuant to the USA PATRIOT Act, amendments to the Bank Secrecy Act and its implementing regulation and investigate name matches as required by law. Dominion does not originate loans which are made to borrowers or guarantors which are specially designated nationals.

State Law

Dominion must comply with applicable state law governing lending practices and are required to maintain applicable state lending licenses to the extent required.

Key Requirements:

  • OFAC & Exclusionary List Screening: All parties must be screened against OFAC's SDN List and the seller's exclusionary list. Borrowers/guarantors from FATF high-risk jurisdictions are ineligible.
  • Fraud & Background Checks: Reports must verify identity, SSN, entity status, criminal records, and property details. High-risk alerts must be addressed and documented in the loan file. If alerts cannot be cleared electronically, an Underwriter's Certification is required.
  • Judgment & Lien Searches: Material judgments or liens must be resolved or documented.
  • Occupancy Verification: Fraud checks must confirm occupancy to support the Business Purpose & Occupancy Affidavit.
  • Unavailable Background Check: If a report cannot be obtained, proof of an unsuccessful return must be provided.
3

General Definitions

Cost Basis

Includes purchase price, closing expenses, acquisition costs (assignment fees capped at 10% of appraised value; excess requires Dominion Financial desk approval), verified rehab amounts, and borrower-paid costs (e.g., taxes, HOA dues, liens).

Assignment Fees

Capped at 10% of the final appraised value. Amounts exceeding this require Dominion Financial desk approval.

Loan to As-Is Value (LTV)

Calculated by dividing the loan amount at origination by the lower of the as-is appraised value or the purchase price.

Loan to Cost (LTC)

Calculated by dividing the loan amount at origination by the borrower's cost basis at origination.

Purchase

Refers to the acquisition of real property for business or investment purposes. LTV calculation uses the lower of appraised value or purchase price. Seller concessions allowed up to 3%, or up to 6% on an exception basis.

Delayed Purchase

Applies to properties purchased within 180 days of the note date without existing debt. LTV calculation uses the lower of original purchase price or current appraised value, except when:

  • A rehab Scope of Work (SOW) budget, including documentation of pre- and post-renovation improvements, is provided.
  • Dominion Financials' internally reconciled valuation supporting the post-rehab value is available; if provided, LTV is capped at 70%. Otherwise, it is capped at the lower of purchase price or appraised value.

Dominion Financial may review property values if a variance greater than 10% is identified.

Refinance

Refers to altering the terms of an existing mortgage (e.g., interest rate, loan term, or equity extraction).

Cash Out Refinance

Defined as a refinance transaction where net proceeds exceed 2% of the loan amount or $2,000, whichever is less. Cash-out refinances are:

  • Ineligible if occurring less than 90 days from the note date.
  • Eligible between 90 and 180 days only if the original HUD and rehab budget/SOW are provided.
  • Subject to maximum cash-out limits as outlined in the LTV Matrix.

Rate/Term Refinance

Defined as a refinance transaction with cash back not exceeding 2% of the loan amount or $2,000, whichever is less. Eligible within 90 days of the note date, adhering to the same cash-back limits.

Experienced Investor (EI)

An individual borrower with at least one year of experience in owning and managing commercial or non-owner occupied residential real estate within the last three years. For multiple borrowers, only one must meet this criterion.

First-Time Investor (FTI)

A borrower who does not meet the Experienced Investor definition but has owned a primary residence for at least one year.

First-Time Home Buyer (FTHB)

Applies when all applicants have never previously owned a residential property (real property ownership interest, either sole or joint) within the three-year period preceding the application date of the security property.

Lien

First lien mortgage only; no additional debt is permitted.

Section 8 and Section 42

  • Eligible on an exception basis and may require up to a 10% LTV reduction.
  • Housing Assistance Payment (HAP) Contract:
    • Issued by the local housing authority confirming the rent amount.
    • Must show the breakdown between tenant-paid rent and Section 8 subsidy.
    • In some cases, DSCR may calculated based off contractual rent to calculate DSCR

Legal- Non-Conforming

  1. Definition:
    • A legal non-conforming property is one that does not meet current zoning requirements but is grandfathered due to previous compliance when originally built.
  2. Eligibility Criteria:
    • Must be grandfathered and legally permitted by local zoning authorities.
    • No known zoning violations or pending zoning changes affecting the property's use.
    • Must be typical for the market and have comparable sales of similar properties.
    • Cannot have restrictions preventing rebuilding if destroyed beyond a certain percentage (e.g., 50%+ of value).
    • Proof from the county must be provided on its rebuild status after a partial or total loss.
    • The property must be able to be rebuilt as it stands with the same structure, unit (s), and likeness after a partial or total loss, or it is not eligible.

NEW YORK – CEMA

Key Financial Ratios

Cost Basis

Includes: Purchase price + closing expenses + acquisition costs (assignment fees capped at 10% of appraised value; with manager exception up to 16%. Any excess beyond requires Dominion Loan Desk approval) + verified rehab amounts + borrower-paid costs (e.g., taxes, HOA dues, liens)

Loan to As-Is Value (LTV)

  • Calculation: Loan amount ÷ the lower of as-is value, CDA/ARR value, or purchase price
  • Maximum: Refer to Appendix A

Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR)

  • Calculation: Monthly gross rental income ÷ monthly PITIA (principal, interest, taxes, insurance, HOA) or ITIA (interest, taxes, insurance, HOA) for interest-only loans.
  • Gross Rental Income: The lower of actual rent (per lease) or 100% market rent (per appraisal, long-term)
  • Minimum DSCR:

Property Requirements

Third Party Appraisal Reports

  • For Loans ≤ $1,500,000:
    • One full appraisal with a market rent estimate
    • ARR or CDA required; a variance greater than –10% necessitates a field review or a second appraisal (with the lower value used)
  • For Loans > $1,500,000:
    • Two full appraisals are required (the lower value is used)
    • ARR/CDA may be waived

1-4 Units

  • Appraisals must be USPAP/FIRREA compliant and dated within 120 days (or 180 days with recertification)
  • In some cases, depending on scope and type of deal, appraisal duration could be capped at 90 days
  • For extreme situations and one-off scenarios, exceptions can be made to go up to 365 days as long as the 1004D is 120 days from closing and Dominion Desk Approval is obtained 🔬

5-10 Unit Appraisals

  • The appraisal must be dated within 120 days of the note date.
  • After 120 days, a new appraisal is required.

Appraisal Completed After Disaster

  • An exterior inspection of the subject property, performed by the original appraiser, if possible, is required
  • The appraiser should provide a statement indicating if the subject property is free from any damage, is in the same condition as the previous inspection, and the marketability and value remain the same
  • Inspection Report must include new photographs of the subject property and street view, through one of the following:
    • FNMA Form 1004D, or
    • Post Disaster Inspection (PDI) Report
  • Any damage must be repaired and re-inspected prior to purchase

Appraisal Completed After Disaster Event

  • The appraiser must comment on the adverse event and certify that there has been no change in the valuation
  • Any damage noted in the appraisal report must be repaired and re-inspected prior to purchase

Deferred Maintenance

General Requirements

  • Properties with evident deferred maintenance may be rejected as loan security or require reduced loan amounts
  • All repairs must be completed with a Satisfactory Completion Certificate and photos included in the loan file
  • Minor cosmetic repairs cannot exceed $5,000 or 3.0% of property value (whichever is greater)
  • All health and safety issues must be resolved before closing

Transaction-Specific Requirements

Refinance Transactions
  • Properties with minor "as-is" deferred maintenance: Clear photo documentation proving completion of repairs prior to closing is acceptable
Purchase Transactions
  • Properties with minor "as-is" deferred maintenance:
    • "Cost-to-cure" estimate required (if not specified by appraiser)
    • Funds covering "cost-to-cure" must be collected at closing
    • Post-closing photographic evidence of repairs required after ownership transfer

  • Rural Property Classification:
  • A property may be classified as rural if it meets one or more of the following:

    • Situated on more than five (5) acres
    • Located in a neighborhood with less than 25% "built-up" area
    • Classified as rural by the appraiser
    • Located in a remote or isolated area
    • Designated as rural or underserved by CFPB, even if the appraisal indicates suburban classification (structure and loan placement will determine final classification)
